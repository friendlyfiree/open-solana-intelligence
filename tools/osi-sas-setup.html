OSI SAS Setup maintainer one-time tool

Creates the two on-chain accounts that the OSI_VERIFIED_ANALYST credential is built on: the OSI Credential account and the Schema account under it, on Solana mainnet, via the Solana Attestation Service (program 22zoJMtdu4tQc2PzL74ZUT7FrwgB1Udec8DdW4yw4BdG, SDK sas-lib). It also creates the dedicated low-privilege issuer key the server later uses for automated issuance.

Read before you sign. This page never asks for, holds, stores, or transmits your wallet's private key. Each on-chain action is one normal Phantom approval that you perform by hand, exactly once. Connect the configured OSI maintainer wallet only (it becomes the credential authority and pays the small rent for the two accounts). No name, case data, or personal information is written on chain: the schema carries only integer tier/status codes.

Two keys, two jobs. Your maintainer wallet is the credential authority: it signs by hand in Phantom and its secret key must never be typed, exported, or stored anywhere, not even on OSI's own server. Automated day-to-day issuance instead uses the separate dedicated issuer key created in step 2: a low-privilege key whose only job is signing OSI_VERIFIED_ANALYST credential issue/revoke transactions, and the only secret this page ever shows. That dedicated secret is the only key that ever goes on the server, so if the server is ever compromised your wallet, your governance authority, and your funds are not, and the issuer key can be replaced from your wallet at any time.
