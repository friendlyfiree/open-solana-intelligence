About OSI

Public intelligence with an auditable boundary.

OSI organizes public Solana incident evidence into attributable, independently reviewed, and challengeable records. It proves who acted, what version they acted on, and which proof was verified. It does not decide automatic truth, guilt, or legal certainty.

Product boundary

Process integrity, without false authority.

OSI is
  • A public evidence and provenance workspace
  • A review protocol with attributable decisions
  • A challengeable record with immutable versions
  • A non-custodial interface for direct wallet actions
OSI is not
  • A recovery service or private investigator
  • A legal verdict, guilt engine, or enforcement authority
  • A custodian, escrow, or platform balance
  • A guarantee of publication, payment, or fund return

Analyst authority

Verify an OSI analyst credential on Solana.

The Solana Attestation Service credential confirms that one wallet currently has OSI review authority under the exact OSI_VERIFIED_ANALYST credential, schema, and issuer. It is not KYC, proof of a person's identity, an endorsement, or proof that a review is correct.

What the badge means
  • The public verifier returned valid with the exact verified state.
  • The answer is tied to the pasted wallet and OSI's on-chain SAS configuration.
  • A missing, expired, invalid, or unavailable credential never earns a badge.
  • Review authority is separate from truth, correctness, identity, or legal certainty.
Public wallet verifier

No wallet connection is needed. OSI displays only the answer returned by the existing public sas_verify endpoint.

Protocol principles

Built to preserve disagreement and history.

01

Private by default

New Cases and pending evidence stay outside public projections until an authorized transition opens them.

02

Exact versions

Reports and reviews bind to immutable content versions. Revision adds history instead of erasing it.

03

Independent authority

Ownership, analyst eligibility, and maintainer authority remain separate. One role cannot silently replace another.

04

Challengeable outcomes

Published process records retain review, challenge, correction, and proof history for public inspection.
