Open intelligence, not a paywall

When SOL leaves your wallet, or an institution's, and you cannot trace it yourself, the usual options are a paid analyst who answers to one client, or silence. OSI runs the opposite way. Anyone can open a case for free; verified analysts investigate, their peers review the work, and the result becomes a permanent public record anyone can check. The reward here is reputation, not resale, and none of it is a promise to recover what was lost.

The method, in the open

no black boxes · every step reproducible in a block explorer

Every attribution here is a chain of evidence anyone can re-walk: anchor on a transaction or a filing, follow the funding, fingerprint the custody, cluster the related accounts, and cross-validate before anything is labeled. Nothing is taken on trust. A wrong call is visible and fixable in the open, not buried in a private dataset.

Confidence is graded, never assumed
Verified

A definitive on-chain link: a direct transfer from a known entity or Foundation wallet, or a transaction the entity itself signed. The strongest tier.

High confidence

Strong evidentiary clustering. Timing, size, custodian fingerprint and disclosure all converge, but no single cryptographic proof of ownership.

Publicly labeled

The entity or its custodian has publicly disclosed the address. We relay it and run an independent sanity check, but the source is their statement.

Where the method stops. Clustering is probabilistic. "High confidence" means the evidence strongly converges, not that ownership is legally proven. Every attribution here can be challenged with better evidence, and that is the point of doing it in the open: a wrong call is visible and fixable, not buried in a private dataset.
