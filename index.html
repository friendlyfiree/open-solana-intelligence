🔬 The Tradecraft

no black boxes · reproduce every step with a block explorer

Wallet attribution can look like magic. It isn't. It is a repeatable chain of evidence anyone can follow with a block explorer and patience. Below is the exact five-step method behind every entry on the registry. Walk it in order, check our work, or run your own.

STEP 01 // ANCHOR ON A DISCLOSURE

Start off-chain, with the filing

Every trace starts off-chain. An SEC 8-K, an 8-K/A, or a press release that states a SOL purchase, a custody arrangement, or a staking deal, with a date and an amount. That gives you a when and a how much to hunt for on the chain.

Example"Helius (HSDT) begins purchasing SOL, 760,190 SOL" on 2025-09-22. Now you know the rough size and the window to look in.
STEP 02 // MATCH THE FUNDING FLOW

Find inflows that line up

Find on-chain inflows whose timing and size match the disclosure. A treasury buying hundreds of thousands of SOL leaves a footprint: large transfers out of an exchange or OTC desk (Coinbase Prime, FalconX) into fresh addresses, clustered around the announced date.

Signala 1M+ SOL transfer arriving directly from a Solana Foundation non-circulating supply wallet is about as strong as on-chain evidence gets.
STEP 03 // FINGERPRINT THE CUSTODIAN

Identify who controls the keys

Custodians leave recognizable on-chain patterns: deposit-address structures, program interactions, and stake-authority layouts that repeat across their clients. Matching those patterns tells you who controls the keys, not just where the coins sit.

TellsCoinbase Prime, Fireblocks, Anchorage and BitGo each route and stake differently. The plumbing gives them away.
STEP 04 // CLUSTER THE ACCOUNTS

Expand one wallet into the full set

Follow each stake account back to its stake authority and withdraw authority; group deposit addresses that share a controller. One confirmed wallet expands into the whole treasury this way: custody wallets, stake accounts, and the validators they delegate to.

Methodshared authorities and repeated counterparties turn a single address into a mapped treasury.
STEP 05 // CROSS-VALIDATE BEFORE YOU LABEL

Two independent lines, or it waits

Never publish on a single signal. Require at least two independent lines, a timing match plus a custodian fingerprint plus a corroborating filing, before attributing an address. Then assign a confidence level, not a verdict.

Ruleif it only checks one box, it stays in the notes, not on the registry.
Confidence is graded, never assumed
Verified

A definitive on-chain link: a direct transfer from a known entity or Foundation wallet, or a transaction the entity itself signed. The strongest tier.

High confidence

Strong evidentiary clustering. Timing, size, custodian fingerprint and disclosure all converge, but no single cryptographic proof of ownership.

Publicly labeled

The entity or its custodian has publicly disclosed the address. We relay it and run an independent sanity check, but the source is their statement.

Where the method stops. Clustering is probabilistic. "High confidence" means the evidence strongly converges, not that ownership is legally proven. Every attribution here can be challenged with better evidence, and that is the point of doing it in the open: a wrong call is visible and fixable, not buried in a private dataset.
